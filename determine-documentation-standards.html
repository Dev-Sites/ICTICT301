The Australian User Documentation Standards

The Australian standard for user documentation is AS4258. It is a methodology or a set of procedures of making user documentation. Out of the three types of standards it falls under the process standards - these are things that tell you how to plan, or how to manage or estimate or assess it. The AS4258 is an Australian Standard that is used for software user documentation and will soon become an international standard.

The documentation unlike its cousin the Australian Standard AS4598. It still tells you how to plan and develop user documentation, but it doesn’t tell you what to put in your manuals, or how to lay them out. The idea is that people call AS4258 from a contract, and if the person responding to the contract doesn’t do what AS4258 says, they can be sued. AS4258 is written to be read in a court of law.

Are the agreed specifications, procedures and or guidelines to be utilised for all documentation

They could be based on:- Industry standards, organisational standards or simply from consumer experience and feedback

Australian Standards (AS) a non-government organisation provides guidelines on accepted standards within Australia

Complying with all Australian copyright and intellectual property laws is critical for all documentation created
